Graphical Abstract

The spectrum of esters: Heteroatom-bridged 1,5-bifunctional organomagnesium reagents enable the direct transformation of carboxylic acid esters into acridinium, xanthylium, and SiR dyes. Owing to the high diversity of esters, the chemical, photophysical, and electrochemical properties of the fluorophores can be ideally modulated to provide organophotoredox catalysts for dual catalysis with nickel and dyes amenable to fluorescence enhancement.

Abstract

Despite the manifold use of heterocyclic fluorophores, only a fraction of the desired dye diversity can be accessed by contemporary synthetic approaches. Herein, we describe a modular method that converts various carboxylic acid esters directly into a broad spectrum of heteroanthrylium fluorophores. The double addition of heteroatom-bridged 1,5-bifunctional organomagnesium reagents to esters leads to the formation of acridinium, xanthylium, and SiR fluorophores after dehydrative acidic work-up. This one-step synthetic method provides access to organophotoredox catalysts for dual catalysis with nickel and dyes amenable to fluorescence enhancement.

In materials science, biological imaging, or chemical synthesis, organic fluorophores have gained fundamental importance in many respects.1 However, recent developments increasingly require fluorophores with tailored photophysical, chemical, and electrochemical properties.2 To address this growing demand, synthetic endeavors frequently resort to the versatility of heterocycle synthesis. The classical electrophilic aromatic substitution reaction (Figure 1 a, left)3 has thus seen great progress from the original harsh conditions and requirement for an additional oxidation step.4 On the other hand, the addition of nucleophiles to heteroanthrones (Figure 1 a, right) represents an elegant alternative for the preparation of complementary fluorophores.4, 5 Nevertheless, both approaches lack the required variability and flexibility to suitably modulate the electrochemical and photophysical properties of fluorophores.

Carboxylic acid esters would be ideal substrates for the versatile construction of heterocycles as they are abundant, stable, and structurally diverse. We hence envisioned that the combination of esters with 1,5-bifunctional organomagnesium reagents6 would enable the direct formation of heterocyclic fluorophores. The double addition of a heteroatom-bridged 1,5-dimetallic dianilide 2 to carboxylic acid esters (1) would form an alcoholate 3, which, upon acidic work-up, undergoes dehydration to give a broad range of distinct fluorophores in a single step (Figure 1 b; acridinium dyes 4: X=NMe; xanthylium dyes 5: X=O; SiR dyes 6: X=SiMe2). Compared with the prototypical electrophilic aromatic substitution, this approach circumvents subsequent oxidation reactions and benefits from the ideal reactivity of 1,5-bifunctional organomagnesium reagents with esters. Owing to the complementarity with heteroanthrone additions and the diversity of readily available carboxylic acid methyl esters, this direct heterocycle synthesis would therefore enable the assembly of a large variety of tailored fluorophores.

To explore the feasibility of this concept, we prepared nitrogen-based reagent 2 a (X=NMe) by a palladium-catalyzed Buchwald–Hartwig amination, N-methylation, and subsequent double metalation with elemental magnesium (see the Supporting Information, Scheme S1).7 With reagent 2 a in hand, we embarked on the direct transformation of esters into acridinium dyes (Table 1). Methyl benzoate was treated with 1.40 equiv of the in situ prepared reagent 2 a (12 h at 60 °C) followed by acidic work-up with aqueous HBr8 to form acridinium bromide 4 a in 87 % yield. To determine the scope of the method, the impact of the ester reactivity was investigated under identical reaction conditions. As expected, higher reactivity was observed for fluorobenzoate than for methoxybenzoate (entries 2 and 3, 70 % vs. 84 %). The sterically demanding methyl 1-naphthoate was also converted in good yield (entry 4, 75 %), and even with remarkably bulky methyl 2,4,6-trimethylbenzoate, the corresponding acridinium fluorophore 4 e was isolated in a yield of 74 % (entry 5). Furthermore, the use of an additional equivalent of reagent 2 a also allowed the conversion of protic methyl anthranilate into acridinium salt 4 f in 73 % yield (entry 6).

We next examined the photophysical and electrochemical properties of fluorophores 4 a–f (see the Supporting Information for details).7 Intriguingly, the diaminoacridinium salts showed redox potentials similar to those of the prevalent transition-metal-based Ir[dF(CF3)ppy]2(dtbbpy)⋅PF6 photoredox catalyst (E1/2(P/P−)=−1.37 V; E1/2(P*/P−)=+1.21 V vs. SCE).9 In contrast to the highly oxidizing Fukuzumi acridinium catalyst (MesMeAcr⋅BF4: E1/2(P/P−)=−0.57 V; E1/2(P*/P−)=+2.06 V vs. SCE),10 an oxidative character particularly well suited for dual catalysis was observed (4 e: E1/2(P/P−)=−1.15 V and E1/2(P*/P−)=+1.25 V vs. SCE).7, 11 Combined with their favorable ground-state reduction potentials, acridinium salts 4 a–f thus engaged in organophotoredox/Ni dual catalysis for the cross-coupling of Boc-l-proline (7) with methyl 4-iodobenzoate (8; Table 2).12 Whereas acridinium salts 4 a–d already indicated efficient formation of the desired product, the mesityl-substituted diaminoacridinium catalyst 4 e gave complete conversion after 3 h (entry 5, 86 % yield). In stark contrast, with aminophenylacridinium bromide 4 f (Φfl=0.006), MesMeAcr⋅BF4, Na2-Eosin Y, without photoredox catalyst, or in reactions performed in the dark, the formation of the arylated product 9 was not observed. The tetramethyldiaminoacridinium salts 4 a–f therefore complement the carbazolyl dicyanobenzene organophotoredox catalysts recently reported by Zhang and co-workers for dual catalysis with nickel.13

Having prepared acridinium dyes for organophotoredox catalysis, we focused on the one-step transformation of esters into oxygen- and silicon-based fluorophores. Aside from the archetypical xanthylium dyes, we centered our attention on long-wavelength-emitting silaheterocycles (X=SiMe2, SiR dyes).14, 15 The corresponding 1,5-bifunctional organomagnesium reagents were expeditiously synthesized in three steps by heteroatom-bridge formation, double bromination, and dimagnesiation (Schemes S2 and S3). Both reagents showed ideal reactivity and remarkable stability even after storage of reagent 2 c for 60 days (RT under argon).7

By using reagents 2 b and 2 c, methyl benzoate was converted into tetramethylrosamine 5 a as well as its SiR analogue 6 a in 85 % and 72 % yield, respectively (Table 3, entries 1 and 7). Esters with differing electronic properties again exhibited the expected reactivity trends (entries 1–3 and 7–9). However, the effect of the increased steric demand of the naphthyl substituent in 5 d was directly noticeable with 2 b (entry 4). Nonetheless, even the capricious alkyl- and alkenyl-substituted xanthylium dyes 5 e and 5 f could be prepared with the 1,5-bifunctional organomagnesium reagent (entries 5 and 6). To access SiR dyes with groups for further diversification, methyl 4-bromobenzoate, dimethyl terephthalate, and even protic methyl anthranilate (by employing an additional equivalent of reagent 2 c) were efficiently transformed into the corresponding SiR fluorophores (entries 10–12, 6 d–f). Furthermore, the robustness of the protocol was corroborated by a tenfold increase of the reaction scale, giving 6 f in remarkably high yield (entry 12; 1.00 mmol, 90 %).7, 16

To obtain activatable fluorophores, we next investigated the fluorescence enhancement by azide formation as reported by Bertozzi.14h, 17 The amino SiR fluorophore 6 f (Φfl=0.002; Scheme 1), which is weakly emitting owing to intramolecular fluorescence self-quenching, was therefore diazotized and treated with NaN3 to obtain azide 6 h, which showed a 100-fold increase in fluorescence quantum yield (83 %, Φfl=0.20). Interestingly, a similar enhancement was also observed by simple acylation of the amino group with Ac2O in aqueous medium (6 g; 84 % yield, Φfl=0.20).7, 17a It was thus confirmed that activatable fluorophores can also be directly accessed from esters with protic groups.

In summary, we have developed a modular transformation of carboxylic acid esters into acridinium, xanthylium, and SiR fluorophores. The employed 1,5-bifunctional organomagnesium reagents are accessible in three steps and enable the high-yielding synthesis of dyes with distinct photophysical properties. Owing to the mild reaction conditions, a particularly broad scope was established, encompassing sensitive products, red-emitting fluorophores for fluorescent enhancement, as well as novel tetramethyldiaminoacridinium organophotoredox catalysts. The high variability of the ester substrates hence enables the direct one-step preparation of tailored fluorophores for biological imaging,18 catalysis, and materials science. Ongoing studies in our group focus on the systematic modulation of the redox properties of organophotoredox catalysts.
